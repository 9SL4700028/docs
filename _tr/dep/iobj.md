The canonical `iobj` form is the dative object. This relation requires the existence of a direct object. However, in Turkish, direct object can be ommitet and the iobj can optionally remain in the sentence. Therefore we do not use this relation.
~~~ sdparse
Alper bana iki elma verdi . \n Alper gave me two apples .
iobj(verdi, bana)
iobj(gave, bana)
~~~
