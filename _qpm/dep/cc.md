A coordinating conjunction `cc` (cc) is the relation between a conjunct and a preceding coordinating conjunction, 
which is a word that links words or larger constituents without syntactically subordinating one to the other and expresses a semantic relationship 
between them.

In Pomak all coordinated conjunct relations are marked under the label cc, which mainly is the coordinating adjunct i (and), ta (and), íli (or), 
as well as the adversatives ála (but), pak (but) and am (but): 

~~~ sdparse

magáreso je stáro i jáko
the donkey is old and strong 
cc (jáko, i)     
conj (stáro, jáko)    
~~~

~~~ sdparse
hódih ta go pýtah 
I went and asked him
cc (pýtah, ta)
conj (hódih, pýtah)  
~~~

~~~ sdparse
magáreso je stáro íli  jáko 
the donkey is (either) old or strong                                      
cc (jáko, íli)     
conj (stáro, jáko) 

magáreso je stáro alá  jáko
the donkey is old but strong
cc (jáko, ála)
conj (stáro, jáko)  
~~~

~~~ sdparse
magáreso je stáro pak  jáko
the donkey is old but strong   
cc (jáko, pak)
conj (stáro, jáko)  
~~~

~~~ sdparse
am ty, kaná só razhóždaš nacýj-nasám?
but you, why do you stroll back and forth ?
lit: but you, what yourself stroll that way - this way ?
cc (razhóždaš, am)
~~~
